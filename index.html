Because sex is something we consider as very private, speaking about sexual practices tends to nourish our fantasies. You may have wondered what the small secret pleasures of your friends, neighbors, or even boss might be. The bad news – though it could be considered good after all – is that unless you try questioning them yourself, you'll never know much more. This is why many clichés are still going strong. Are there really fewer homosexuals amongst Catholics? Is sodomy more frequent among left-wingers or right-wingers? Go ahead and test your a priori on sex with total impunity. And we promise not to laugh, even if you're wrong on all counts!

These non-cohabiting couples who are more satisfied than the others — our friends the reasearchers call them living apart together (LAT ) — are very nice, but what about single people ? Among couples (LAT or not), one out of five respondents are little or not satisfied. Among single people — whether confirmed in their singlehood or having already been in a relationship — we go up to one out of two ! The mutual trust and shared intimacy we experience with the same familiar sexual partner seems to be essential to our well-being. People in a relationship claim to be far less unsatisfied than single people. So, to live happily, live apart... but in a relationship !
